06 / FAQ

Things teams ask us in the first call.

How long does a typical iCAFFE rollout take?

+

A focused forwarder deployment — quoting, booking, documents, tracking — is live in 8–12 weeks for most mid-size operations. Larger multi-branch rollouts with ERP and customs integrations typically run 16–20 weeks, phased by branch.

Can we deploy iCAFFE on-prem or does it have to be cloud?

+

Both. iCAFFE runs on our managed cloud, on customer-owned cloud accounts (AWS, Azure, GCP), or entirely on-premise for customers with data-residency or air-gap requirements.

Which carriers and customs authorities are integrated out of the box?

+

60+ direct integrations including IATA ONE Record, Cargo-XML, CHAMP, CCN, ICEGATE (India), SARS (South Africa), EU NCTS, and major air & ocean carriers. Custom integrations are added as first-class modules.

How does pricing work?

+

iCAFFE is priced by shipment volume tier and modules enabled. There are no per-seat user fees for operational staff. Enterprise plans include a committed uptime SLA, named support and quarterly business reviews.

What about data security?

+

iCAFFE is SOC 2 Type II audited, ISO 27001 certified, and GDPR-compliant. Data is encrypted at rest and in transit. SSO, RBAC, and full audit trails are standard on every plan.

Can iCAFFE work alongside systems we already have?

+

Yes. Most customers start by connecting iCAFFE to an existing ERP or TMS. Over time, iCAFFE typically becomes the operational system of record while the ERP settles into its natural role in finance and controlling.
